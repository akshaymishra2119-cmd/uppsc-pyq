### 1. Polity & Governance

---

#### 1.1 Criminalisation of Politics: ADR Report on Rajya Sabha

**Summary:**
The Association for Democratic Reforms (ADR) and National Election Watch (NEW) released a report revealing that 31% of sitting Rajya Sabha MPs have declared criminal cases against them, with 16% facing serious criminal charges. The findings highlight the deepening criminalisation of Indian politics and raise concerns about legislative credibility.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (Indian Polity, Parliament, Electoral Reforms)
- **Prelims Keywords:** ADR (Association for Democratic Reforms), National Election Watch (NEW), Rajya Sabha, criminalisation of politics, Representation of the People Act
- **Mains Angle:** *"The criminalisation of politics undermines democratic values and governance quality. Critically examine the causes, consequences, and possible remedies."*

---

#### 1.2 Air Suvidha 2.0 Launched for Ebola-Affected Travellers

**Summary:**
India reactivated the Air Suvidha portal as **Air Suvidha 2.0** on 25 June 2026, for passengers arriving from or transiting through Ebola-affected countries. This is a health declaration portal that enables real-time monitoring of incoming travellers for disease surveillance.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (Governance, Health Policy) | GS-3 (Health & Disaster Management)
- **Prelims Keywords:** Air Suvidha 2.0, Ebola virus disease, health surveillance, port of entry screening, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare
- **Mains Angle:** *"How do digital governance tools like the Air Suvidha portal strengthen India's public health infrastructure? Discuss with reference to pandemic preparedness."*

#### 2.1 RBI Monetary Policy: Repo Rate Held at 5.25%

**Summary:**
The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) kept the policy **repo rate unchanged at 5.25%** in its June 2026 meeting. RBI Governor **Sanjay Malhotra** cited risks from the West Asia conflict, elevated energy prices, supply-chain disruptions, and weather-related uncertainties. India's **forex reserves stand at USD 682.3 billion** (as of May 29, 2026), providing ~11 months of import cover.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Indian Economy, Monetary Policy, Inflation)
- **Prelims Keywords:** Repo rate (5.25%), RBI Governor Sanjay Malhotra, CPI inflation (projected 5.1% for 2026-27), GDP growth (projected 6.6%), Forex reserves (USD 682.3 billion), Monetary Policy Committee (MPC)
- **Mains Angle:** *"Examine the challenges faced by the RBI in balancing inflation control with growth support in the context of global energy price volatility."*

### 3. Environment

---

#### 3.1 NISAR Satellite in Science Operations Phase

**Summary:**
The **NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar (NISAR)** satellite, launched on 30 July 2025, entered its **science operations phase** in January 2026. In February 2026, India's NRSC used NISAR data to create **100m-resolution soil moisture maps** for central India and the Indo-Gangetic Plains. NISAR uses L-band (NASA) and S-band (ISRO) radar to track Earth surface changes to centimetre-level precision. Its data is freely accessible globally.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Science & Technology, Environment, Space)
- **Prelims Keywords:** NISAR satellite, NASA-ISRO collaboration, Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR), L-band and S-band radar, NRSC, soil moisture mapping, Indo-Gangetic Plains, Satish Dhawan Space Centre
- **Mains Angle:** *"Space-based Earth observation technologies like NISAR can significantly enhance India's environmental governance. Discuss."*

### 4. Security

---

#### 4.1 End of Naxal Insurgency — India Declares Victory

**Summary:**
The Indian government declared **victory over the Naxalite insurgency** in March 2026 after **Operation Kagar**, a sustained campaign spearheaded by Chhattisgarh police and central forces. The **red corridor**, once covering ~180 districts at its peak, has shrunk to just **3 districts**. All major cadres have been neutralised, killed, or surrendered. Two commanders remain at large.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Internal Security, Left-Wing Extremism)
- **Prelims Keywords:** Operation Kagar, Naxal insurgency, red corridor, Chhattisgarh, left-wing extremism (LWE), Ministry of Home Affairs, Vijay Sharma, Amit Shah
- **Mains Angle:** *"The near-elimination of Left-Wing Extremism in India represents both a security and a development success. Critically evaluate the strategies that led to this outcome."*

---

#### 4.2 DRDO Hands Over NETRA AEW&C System to IAF

**Summary:**
DRDO formally handed over the **Final Operational Clearance (FOC)** certificate of the indigenous **'Netra' Airborne Early Warning and Control (AEW&C) system** to the Indian Air Force. Netra functions as a flying radar station, detecting and tracking airborne and maritime threats well beyond the range of ground-based radars.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Defence Technology, Internal Security, Science & Technology)
- **Prelims Keywords:** DRDO, Netra AEW&C, Airborne Early Warning and Control System, Final Operational Clearance (FOC), Indian Air Force, indigenisation
- **Mains Angle:** *"Discuss the strategic importance of indigenously developed Airborne Early Warning and Control Systems in the context of India's evolving security doctrine."*

---

#### 5.1 ISRO Space Hackathon 2026

**Summary:**
ISRO launched the **Indian Space Hackathon 2026**, inviting undergraduate, postgraduate, and PhD students to solve real-world space challenges including satellite analysis, AI-based climate modelling, and space technology. One challenge focuses on creating an **AI-powered digital twin of India's climate system** for improved environmental forecasting.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Space Technology, Digital India, AI)
- **Prelims Keywords:** ISRO Space Hackathon 2026, digital twin, AI in climate modelling, TRISHNA mission, thermal imaging
- **Mains Angle:** *"How can innovation-driven platforms like space hackathons bridge the gap between academic talent and national technological goals in India?"*

#### 5.2 TRISHNA Mission (Upcoming)

**Summary:**
India's upcoming **TRISHNA mission** (a joint ISRO-CNES venture, scheduled 2026) will provide high-resolution thermal imaging of land and coastal regions. It will monitor **crop water stress, irrigation, glaciers, urban ecosystems**, and climate-related changes — directly supporting India's water security and agricultural planning.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Space, Agriculture, Environment)
- **Prelims Keywords:** TRISHNA, thermal imaging, ISRO-CNES, crop water stress, irrigation monitoring, glacier monitoring
- **Mains Angle:** *"Space technologies have become indispensable for agricultural planning and water resource management. Illustrate with recent examples."*

### 6. International Relations

---

#### 6.1 PM Modi's Europe Tour — France, Slovakia, G7 Summit

**Summary:**
PM Narendra Modi visited **France** and **Slovakia** in June 2026, followed by participation in the **G7 Summit at Évian-les-Bains, France**. The India-France partnership was expanded to include AI, digital innovation, nuclear energy, and maritime security, with an agreement to **double bilateral trade in 5 years**. The Slovakia visit was the **first by any Indian PM since 1993**, elevating ties to a **Comprehensive Partnership**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (India's Foreign Policy, International Relations)
- **Prelims Keywords:** G7 Summit 2026 (Évian-les-Bains), India-France strategic partnership, India-Slovakia Comprehensive Partnership, Atmanirbhar Bharat, critical minerals, maritime security
- **Mains Angle:** *"India's deepening engagement with Europe reflects a maturing multi-alignment strategy. Examine the significance of recent Indo-European diplomatic developments."*

### 7. Geopolitics

---

#### 7.1 G7 Summit 2026 — Key Outcomes

**Summary:**
The **G7 Summit (June 15-17, 2026, France)** produced landmark decisions on multiple fronts: (1) welcomed a **US-Iran nuclear deal** that prevents Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons; (2) agreed on a **France-UK-led multinational initiative** to restore maritime traffic in the **Strait of Hormuz**; (3) reaffirmed **support for Ukraine** with pledges of air defence systems and long-range capabilities; (4) reiterated opposition to **status quo changes in the South China Sea, East China Sea, and Taiwan Strait**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (International Organisations, Foreign Policy, Geopolitics)
- **Prelims Keywords:** G7 2026 (Évian-les-Bains), Strait of Hormuz, Iran nuclear deal, Ukraine air defence, South China Sea, Taiwan Strait, G7 leaders' statement
- **Mains Angle:** *"The G7's 2026 summit reflects both the convergence and divergence of interests among advanced democracies. Analyse the implications for the emerging world order."*

---

#### 7.2 US-Iran Nuclear Deal

**Summary:**
A landmark **US-Iran nuclear agreement** was announced around the time of the G7 Summit. The deal, described as "historic" by G7 leaders, aims to prevent Iran from acquiring any nuclear weapon and addresses threats related to Iran's **regional and ballistic missile activities**. The deal is also linked to efforts to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz** for global maritime trade.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (International Relations, Nuclear Non-Proliferation) | GS-3 (Energy Security)
- **Prelims Keywords:** Iran nuclear deal 2026, Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT), Strait of Hormuz, ballistic missile programme, JCPOA, energy security, West Asia
- **Mains Angle:** *"A renewed Iran nuclear deal could reshape West Asian geopolitics and global energy markets. Assess its implications for India."*

### 8. Global Economy

---

#### 8.1 World Bank Cuts Global Growth Forecast to 2.5%

**Summary:**
The **World Bank** downgraded its 2026 global growth forecast to **2.5%** (from earlier projections), citing the **Middle East conflict** and energy price shock. The **IMF's reference forecast** projects global growth at 3.1%, contingent on a short-lived conflict, with global **headline inflation at 4.4%**. The MENA region faces the sharpest downward revision (~3 percentage points). In a worst-case scenario (energy crisis + financial market shock), global growth could fall to **1.3%**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Indian Economy, International Trade, Global Financial Institutions)
- **Prelims Keywords:** World Bank, IMF World Economic Outlook (April 2026), global growth 2.5% / 3.1%, MENA region, energy crisis, debt-to-GDP, WTO, trade war
- **Mains Angle:** *"How do geopolitically driven energy shocks translate into macroeconomic vulnerabilities for emerging economies like India? Discuss with recent evidence."*

#### 9.1 NISAR's Global Climate Monitoring Role

**Summary:**
NISAR's freely accessible data is being used globally for tracking **deforestation, wetland changes, glacier retreat, permafrost thaw, and forest carbon stock**. Its millimetre-precision measurements make it uniquely suited for monitoring **land subsidence, earthquake deformations**, and climate-induced ecosystem shifts, contributing directly to global climate science under frameworks like the **UNFCCC**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Environment, Science & Technology) | GS-2 (International Cooperation on Climate)
- **Prelims Keywords:** NISAR, forest biomass, carbon stock, glacier monitoring, permafrost, wetlands, UNFCCC, Synthetic Aperture Radar, open-access data

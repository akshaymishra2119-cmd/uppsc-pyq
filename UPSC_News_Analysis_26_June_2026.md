## 🇮🇳 INDIA

### 1. POLITY & GOVERNANCE

---

#### 1.1 West Bengal Joins Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY

**Summary:** West Bengal became the **36th State/UT** to implement the Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB PM-JAY) after signing an MoU with the National Health Authority (NHA). The scheme offers health cover of ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (Government policies and interventions, Social sector — Health)
- **Prelims Keywords:** AB PM-JAY, National Health Authority (NHA), ₹5 lakh health cover, 36th State/UT
- **Mains Angle:** "Cooperative federalism in health governance: What does West Bengal's late adoption of AB PM-JAY reveal about Centre-State relations in social welfare schemes?"

---

#### 1.2 Index of Services Production (ISP) Launch

**Summary:** The Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI) is set to launch the **Index of Services Production (ISP)** in July 2026 to track short-term changes in the services sector, which has contributed over **50% of India's GVA** since 2013–14. The ISP will be a periodic indicator similar to the Index of Industrial Production (IIP) for manufacturing.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (Government machinery), GS-3 (Indian Economy — statistics, measurement)
- **Prelims Keywords:** MoSPI, ISP, IIP (Index of Industrial Production), GVA (Gross Value Added), 50% services share
- **Mains Angle:** "How does the launch of the Index of Services Production address the long-standing gap in India's economic data architecture, and what are its implications for monetary and fiscal policy?"

---

#### 1.3 e-Jagriti Wins National e-Governance Award

**Summary:** **e-Jagriti**, the Department of Consumer Affairs' digital consumer grievance redressal platform (launched 2025), won the **Silver Award at the National Awards for e-Governance (NAeG) 2026**, recognising its impact in enabling citizens to file and track complaints against service providers.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (Governance, e-Governance, citizen-centric administration)
- **Prelims Keywords:** e-Jagriti, Department of Consumer Affairs, NAeG 2026, Silver Award
- **Mains Angle:** "Evaluate the role of digital grievance redressal platforms in strengthening consumer rights and improving last-mile governance."

### 2. ECONOMY

---

#### 2.1 RBI Holds Repo Rate at 5.25%; Cuts Growth Forecast

**Summary:** The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) kept the **repo rate unchanged at 5.25%** for the second consecutive monetary policy meeting while revising the GDP growth forecast downward to **6.6% from 6.9%** and raising the inflation projection to **5.1% from 4.6%**. The RBI maintained a "neutral" stance. Analysts flag emerging **stagflationary risks** from elevated global energy prices and supply chain constraints.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Indian Economy — monetary policy, inflation, banking)
- **Prelims Keywords:** Repo rate (5.25%), neutral stance, stagflation, MPC (Monetary Policy Committee), GDP forecast 6.6%, inflation 5.1%
- **Mains Angle:** "In the context of simultaneous growth slowdown and rising inflation, critically examine the limitations of India's monetary policy tools in 2026."

---

#### 2.2 India-US Trade Deal and FII Outflows

**Summary:** A bilateral **India-US trade deal** (signed February 2026) reduced "reciprocal" tariffs on Indian goods from **25% to 18%**, bringing India's effective rate in line with most Asian peers (15–19%). Despite this, **$13.7 billion in foreign capital** (equity) has left India in 2026. To attract flows, the government exempted **Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs)** and the **Bank for International Settlements (BIS)** from capital gains tax and removed NRI/OCI investment limits in government securities.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Indian Economy — trade, capital flows, foreign investment)
- **Prelims Keywords:** India-US trade deal, 18% tariff, FII outflow, BIS, capital gains tax exemption, NRI/OCI
- **Mains Angle:** "Analyse how protectionist trends in global trade and FII volatility are shaping India's macroeconomic management strategy in 2026."

---

#### 2.3 India's Demographic Transition: TFR Falls to 1.9

**Summary:** India's **Total Fertility Rate (TFR) has fallen to 1.9**, below the replacement level of 2.1, signalling a demographic transition. This raises concerns about future population ageing, shrinking workforce, and increased burden on pension and healthcare systems — even as India currently enjoys a **demographic dividend**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-1 (Population and associated issues), GS-2 (Health and social sector), GS-3 (Demographic dividend)
- **Prelims Keywords:** TFR = 1.9, Replacement level = 2.1, Demographic transition, Ageing population, Demographic dividend
- **Mains Angle:** "India's demographic dividend is time-bound. Discuss the policy imperatives of a falling TFR for labour markets, social security, and long-term economic growth."

### 3. ENVIRONMENT

---

#### 3.1 Monsoon Deficit and El Niño Effects

**Summary:** Nearly **75% of India** is experiencing deficient rainfall as of late June 2026, attributed to El Niño conditions and unfavourable atmospheric circulation. The deficit threatens Kharif sowing, rural incomes, and reservoir levels, though expanded renewable energy infrastructure and water conservation measures provide some resilience.

- **GS Paper:** GS-1 (Climate and weather systems — monsoon), GS-3 (Agriculture, Disaster management)
- **Prelims Keywords:** El Niño, Kharif season, IMD, Southwest Monsoon, monsoon deficit, reservoir levels
- **Mains Angle:** "How does El Niño-induced monsoon variability challenge India's agricultural economy, and what structural reforms can reduce this vulnerability?"

### 4. INTERNAL SECURITY

---

#### 4.1 India Declared Free of Naxal Violence — A Historic Milestone

**Summary:** The **Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA)** officially communicated to nine state governments in April 2026 that **no district in India** remains classified as Naxal violence-affected — a landmark milestone after five decades of Left Wing Extremism (LWE) insurgency that claimed **over 17,000 lives**. However, **37 "Legacy and Thrust Districts"** and **1 "District of Concern"** continue to receive focused development attention. The CRPF expanded Forward Operating Bases and security-related expenditure rose over **20% to ₹3,610.80 crore**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Internal security — Left Wing Extremism), GS-2 (Government schemes)
- **Prelims Keywords:** LWE, Red Corridor → Growth Corridor, MHA, Security Related Expenditure (SRE), Special Infrastructure Scheme, ₹3,610.80 crore, 37 Legacy Districts, CRPF
- **Mains Angle:** "Critically examine the factors behind the decimation of Left Wing Extremism in India and the challenges that remain in fully integrating former Red Corridor regions into the national mainstream."

### 5. SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY

---

#### 5.1 ISRO Space Hackathon 2026 and IN-SPACe Student Satellites

**Summary:** ISRO launched the **Indian Space Hackathon 2026** targeting undergraduate, PG, and PhD students, with **15 problem statements** including an AI-powered digital twin of India's climate system. Registration is open until July 1, 2026. Separately, **IN-SPACe** has authorised 17 student satellites/payloads, of which **11 are already in orbit**. AICTE has approved a **Space Technology Minor Course** and ₹10 crore is earmarked annually for space science education outreach.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Science and Technology — space, education), GS-2 (Government initiatives)
- **Prelims Keywords:** ISRO, IN-SPACe, Indian Space Hackathon 2026, AICTE Space Technology Minor Course, AI digital twin, 11 student satellites
- **Mains Angle:** "Discuss the significance of India's push to integrate space science into mainstream education through ISRO, IN-SPACe, and AICTE initiatives."

---

#### 5.2 ISRO–Jal Shakti MoU: Satellite Technology for Water Management

**Summary:** ISRO and the **Ministry of Jal Shakti** signed an MoU to deploy satellite technology across **24 priority areas** for improved water resource management — including groundwater mapping, reservoir monitoring, and flood forecasting. This aligns with India's National Water Policy goals.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Science and Technology — space applications, water management), GS-2 (Government schemes)
- **Prelims Keywords:** ISRO, Jal Shakti, National Water Policy, remote sensing, groundwater mapping, 24 priority areas
- **Mains Angle:** "How can satellite-based earth observation transform India's approach to water security? Discuss with reference to the ISRO-Jal Shakti collaboration."

### 6. INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS

---

#### 6.1 India's BRICS Chairmanship 2026 — Tensions and Opportunities

**Summary:** India holds the **BRICS Chair in 2026** and hosted the **BRICS Foreign Ministers' Meeting** in June. However, **divisions over the Middle East conflict** (specifically India's refusal to criticise Israeli strikes on Iran — a BRICS member) blocked consensus. The US Secretary of State Marco Rubio visited India, launching the **Critical Minerals Framework** and attending a **Quad Foreign Ministers' Meeting**. India also upgraded ties with **Vietnam** to an **Enhanced Comprehensive Strategic Partnership**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (International Relations — BRICS, Quad, bilateral relations)
- **Prelims Keywords:** BRICS Chair 2026, Critical Minerals Framework (India-US), Quad Foreign Ministers, Enhanced Comprehensive Strategic Partnership (India-Vietnam), Marco Rubio visit
- **Mains Angle:** "India's BRICS chairmanship in 2026 tests its strategic autonomy. Critically examine how India is navigating tensions between its Quad commitments and BRICS responsibilities."

---

#### 6.2 India Proposes BRICS Space Economy Framework

**Summary:** India proposed a **BRICS Space Economy Framework** at the BRICS meetings to deepen cooperation among member nations in space technology, earth observation, and satellite data sharing — positioning India's growing space capabilities as a tool for South-South cooperation.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (International Relations), GS-3 (Science and Technology — space)
- **Prelims Keywords:** BRICS Space Economy Framework, South-South cooperation, IN-SPACe, space diplomacy
- **Mains Angle:** "Evaluate India's use of space diplomacy as a strategic instrument within multilateral groupings like BRICS."

### 7. GEOPOLITICS

---

#### 7.1 G7 Évian Summit (June 15–17, 2026)

**Summary:** The **G7 Summit** was held in **Évian-les-Bains, France**, with leaders from Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, the UK, and the US plus the EU. Key outcomes included **reaffirmed support for Ukraine** (increased air defence and long-range capabilities), and attempted consensus on **AI governance and trade**. However, widening divergences on China policy, NATO commitments, and the **US-Israel-Iran conflict** (which began February 28, 2026) constrained joint action. The summit's theme centred on "middle power" relevance in an era of great power competition.

- **GS Paper:** GS-2 (International Relations — multilateral groupings), GS-3 (Global economy, defence)
- **Prelims Keywords:** G7 Évian 2026, G7+EU, Ukraine support, AI governance, US-Israel-Iran conflict (Feb 28, 2026), NATO, middle powers
- **Mains Angle:** "In an era of multipolar competition, evaluate the continued relevance of the G7 as a global governance institution."

#### 8.1 Goldman Sachs Revises India Growth Forecast Downward

**Summary:** **Goldman Sachs** revised India's near-term growth projection to **5.9%** (from ~7%), citing FII outflows and global headwinds, though it expects **6.9% growth for full-year 2026** and **6.8% in 2027**. Global supply chain constraints and high energy prices from the Iran conflict are cited as key drags.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Indian Economy, global economic developments)
- **Prelims Keywords:** Goldman Sachs forecast, India GDP 5.9%, global supply chain, energy prices, FII
- **Mains Angle:** "How do external global shocks — energy price spikes, supply chain disruptions, and capital outflows — limit India's growth potential despite strong domestic fundamentals?"

### 9. GLOBAL ENVIRONMENT

---

#### 9.1 Bonn Climate Talks (UNFCCC SBI/SBSTA 64) — Science Under Attack

**Summary:** The **64th sessions of UNFCCC's SBI and SBSTA** (June 8–18, Bonn) saw dozens of countries call out **"coordinated attacks on climate science"**, undermining the role of evidence-based policymaking. Talks prepared draft decisions for **COP 31 in Antalya, Türkiye (November 2026)**. The UN Secretary-General, in a special address on **June 23**, linked the climate crisis to fossil fuel dependence and flagged the **environmental impact of AI energy use** and the urgency of **methane emission reductions**.

- **GS Paper:** GS-3 (Environment — climate change, international agreements), GS-2 (International organisations)
- **Prelims Keywords:** UNFCCC SBI/SBSTA, Bonn Climate Talks, COP 31 Antalya, Triple-COP year 2026, CBD COP-17 Yerevan, methane emissions, AI energy footprint
- **Mains Angle:** "Discuss the significance of the 2026 'Triple-COP' year for global climate and biodiversity governance. What are India's stakes?"

---

#### 9.2 IPCC Special Report on Climate Change and Cities

**Summary:** The **IPCC's Special Report on Climate Change and Cities** (second draft) is under expert review (May 8 – July 3, 2026). The report is expected to highlight the **disproportionate climate vulnerability of urban centres**, especially in the Global South, and inform Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) and urban planning policy.

- **GS Paper:** GS-1 (Urbanisation), GS-3 (Environment — climate change), GS-2 (International bodies)
- **Prelims Keywords:** IPCC Special Report on Cities, NDCs, Urban Heat Islands, Global South vulnerability, climate-resilient cities
- **Mains Angle:** "How can India's urban planning frameworks be realigned with IPCC findings on climate-vulnerable cities to build long-term resilience?"
